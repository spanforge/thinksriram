## The verse

::verse
Naari Stanaabhara Naabhidesham,
Drishtvaa Maagaa Mohaavisham
Etan Maamsavasaadi Vikaaaram,
Manasi Vichintaya Vaaram Vaaram
::

## Transliteration

Naari stana-bhara naabhi-desham / Drishtvaa maagaa mohaavisham / Etan maamsavasaadi vikaaram / Manasi vichintaya vaaram vaaram

## Translation

Do not be captivated by physical form — it is flesh, fat, and impermanence. Reflect on this again and again.

## The teaching

Shankara addresses sensory infatuation — the compulsive pull of physical attraction. He is not condemning love or beauty; he is pointing to the difference between conscious appreciation and unconscious captivity. The body is impermanent. When we mistake the physical form for the entire person, we build attachment on a foundation that must crumble.

## Living this today

Every age builds its own beauty-obsession machine, and ours is unusually sophisticated. Changing standards, endless comparison, the body you are told you should have instead of the one you were given. Shankara's question is simple: are you choosing consciously, or being chosen by your compulsions? This applies beyond romantic attraction — it applies to anything beautiful we grasp at. The new possession. The bigger house. The title that looks good to others. Reflect on what you chase and why. The object of desire changes across centuries; the restless wanting remains exactly the same. What you chase always changes shape. What chases you never does.
