## The verse

::verse
Baalas Taavat Kriidaasaktaha,
Tarunas Taavat Taruniisaktaha
Vriddhasthaavat Chintaasaktaha,
Parame Brahmani Ko'pi Na Saktaha
::

## Transliteration

Baalas taavat kriidaa-saktaha / Tarunas taavat tarunii-saktaha / Vriddhas-taavat chintaa-saktaha / Parame brahmani ko'pi na saktaha

## Translation

In childhood, one is attached to play; in youth, to romance; in old age, to worry. No one is ever attached to the Supreme Brahman.

## The teaching

Life passes in a relay of attachments, each replacing the previous. The child plays. The youth loves. The old person worries. And through it all, no one pauses to enquire into the nature of existence itself. Every stage of life is consumed by the contents of that stage — and the deeper inquiry never happens.

## Living this today

Replace the stages: in your twenties, you are attached to social validation. In your thirties, to career milestones. In your forties, to financial security. In your fifties, to health anxieties. The objects change; the compulsive attachment does not. And the deeper question — what is this life actually for? — never gets asked because there is always something more urgent. Shankara's invitation is not to abandon these stages but to hold them lightly. Build into your life a daily moment of the larger inquiry. Five minutes of stillness. A journal question: what truly matters? The urgent will always crowd out the important unless you protect the important. The question that matters most is the one you keep postponing.
