## The verse

::verse
Moodha Jahihi Dhanagamatrishnam,
Kuru Sadbuddhim Manasi Vitrishnam
Yallabhase Nijakarmopaattam,
Vittam Tena Vinodaya Chittam
::

## Transliteration

Moodha jahihi dhanagama-trishnam / Kuru sadbuddhim manasi vitrishnam / Yallabhase nijakarmopaattam / Vittam tena vinodaya chittam

## Translation

O fool, give up the thirst for accumulating wealth. Cultivate wisdom in your mind and be contented. Be happy with what you earn through your own righteous effort.

## The teaching

Shankara identifies the root of human suffering: the endless hunger for more money. The mind in the grip of greed is never satisfied. A thousand becomes ten thousand becomes a crore, yet the feeling of "enough" never arrives. True wisdom is knowing when you have sufficient — and finding contentment in what is honestly earned.

## Living this today

In an age of get-rich schemes and constant comparison, this verse is a radical act. Human beings have always measured themselves against their neighbour — someone richer, someone who "made it." That habit profits no one but feeds on your dissatisfaction. Shankara prescribes a counter-move: contentment with honest effort. This does not mean mediocrity. It means your ambition should serve your life, not consume it. Audit your wants this week. How many are genuine needs, and how many are borrowed desires — inherited from someone else's life, not your own? Enough is never a number. It is a decision.
