## The verse

::verse
Angam Galitam Palitam Mundam,
Dashana Viheenim Jaatam Tundam
Vriddho Yaati Griheetve Dandam,
Tadapi Na Munchatyaashaa Pindam
::

## Transliteration

Angam galitam palitam mundam / Dashana viheenim jaatam tundam / Vriddho yaati griheetve dandam / Tadapi na munchaty-aashaa pindam

## Translation

The body has become feeble, the head is grey, the mouth has lost its teeth, the old man walks with a staff — yet still he does not release the bundle of his desires.

## The teaching

A vivid, almost wry image: the ancient man, body failing in every way, still clutching his desires. Shankara renders this with gentle irony. The tragedy is not that we age — it is that we age without growing wise. The capacity for desire outlasts the body's capacity to fulfil it, producing only suffering.

## Living this today

Look around and you will see this pattern at every age. People accumulating possessions they have no time to enjoy. Collecting experiences for the memory they will show others rather than the memory itself. Working overtime on a career that has no room left for actual living. The desire machine has no off switch built in. You have to install one yourself. The question to ask is not "how do I get more?" but "how much is enough?" Draw that line. Everything beyond it is the old man's staff — something to lean on that does not actually take you anywhere. More is not a finish line. It is a habit with no destination.
