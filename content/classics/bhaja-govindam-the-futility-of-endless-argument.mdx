## The verse

::verse
Na Stree Na Naree Na Yauvana Lobhah,
Na Me Manye Kaschit Tatra Shubhah
Na Me Dveshah Na Me Raagah,
Brahmadau Na Me Moho Lagnam
::

## Transliteration

Na stree na naree na yauvana lobhah / Na me manye kaschit tatra shubhah / Na me dveshah na me raagah / Brahmaadau na me moho lagnam

## Translation

I am neither man nor woman, neither youth nor the passion for it. I have no hatred, no attachment. I am not bound by delusion regarding Brahman or anything else.

## The teaching

This verse moves into the voice of the liberated being. It is spoken from the standpoint of pure awareness, which is beyond gender, age, preference, and identity. It is not a denial of these categories but a recognition that one's deepest nature transcends them.

## Living this today

Identity is a great obsession of our age — and rightly so, because the erasure of identities has caused immense historical harm. But Shankara points to a layer beneath identity: the awareness in which all identities arise. You are a woman, a citizen of some country, a professional, a parent — and you are also the awareness that knows all these designations. The latter cannot be threatened, dismissed, or made redundant. When you are grounded in that deeper layer, you can hold your social identities with lightness — advocating fiercely for justice while not being existentially destabilised when your identity is challenged. You are not the label. You are the one who notices it was given to you.
