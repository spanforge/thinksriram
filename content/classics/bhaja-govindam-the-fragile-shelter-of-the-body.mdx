## The verse

::verse
Agre Vahnih Prishthe Bhaanuh,
Raatrau Chubukasamarpita Jaanuh
Karatalabhiksha Stharutalavaasah,
Tadapi Na Munchatyaashaapaasah
::

## Transliteration

Agre vahnih prishthe bhaanuh / Raatrau chubuka-samarpita jaanuh / Karatalab-hiksha starutala-vaasah / Tadapi na munchaty-aashaa-paasah

## Translation

Fire in front, sun behind, sleeping with knees pressed to chin at night, begging food with cupped hands, sleeping under trees — yet the noose of desire does not loosen.

## The teaching

Even extreme ascetics — those who have voluntarily embraced physical hardship and renounced material comfort — can be trapped by subtler desires: the desire for spiritual attainment, for respect, for liberation itself. Desire is not only about money or pleasure. It can attach to anything, including the pursuit of its own elimination.

## Living this today

This is the deepest warning in the text, and the most applicable to self-improvement culture today. You can optimise your morning routine, meditate daily, eat clean, journal faithfully, and still be completely in the grip of desire — the desire to be the kind of person who has their life together. The productivity cult, the wellness industry, the self-help ecosystem — all can become forms of grasping. The question is whether your practices are serving genuine growth or feeding a more refined form of the same restlessness. Practice without attachment to the result of the practice. Even the pursuit of peace can become another way of refusing it.
