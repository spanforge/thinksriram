## The verse

::verse
Sukhato Kriyate Raamaa Bhogah,
Pashchaad Dhanta Shareere Rogah
Yadyapi Loke Maranam Sharanam,
Tadapi Na Munchatyaashaaviranam
::

## Transliteration

Sukhato kriyate raamaa bhogah / Pashchaad dhanta shareere rogah / Yadyapi loke maranam sharanam / Tadapi na munchaty-aashaa-viranam

## Translation

Physical pleasures are enjoyed with delight, but later they bring disease to the body. Even though death is the universal refuge, the archer of desire does not release his arrow.

## The teaching

Every pleasure has a cost — often paid later in the body. Yet knowing this, we pursue pleasures compulsively. And even death, which all of us know is coming, does not shake loose our attachments. Shankara images desire as an archer who never stops shooting even when the target is gone.

## Living this today

Lifestyle diseases driven by excess — metabolic disorders, stress-related illness, burnout, addiction — are a civilisational crisis in every affluent age, ours included. We know the sugar is harmful. We know the screen time is eroding our focus. We know the overwork is unsustainable. And we continue anyway. The information is not the problem — everyone knows what is good for them. The problem is the gap between knowing and living. This is what Shankara is addressing: the structural irrationality of human desire. The solution is not more information but a direct, visceral encounter with consequence — meditation, therapy, honest reflection with trusted people. Make the abstract cost real. Knowing what is good for you was never the hard part. Living it is.
