## The verse

::verse
Arthamanartham Bhaavaya Nityam,
Naasthi Tatah Sukhalesah Satyam
Putraadhapi Dhana Bhaajaam Bheetih,
Sarvatra Esha Vihaati Reetih
::

## Transliteration

Artham-anartham bhaavaya nityam / Naasthi tatah sukhalesah satyam / Putraadhapi dhana bhaajaam bheetih / Sarvatra esha vihaati reetih

## Translation

Reflect constantly that wealth is fraught with evil. There is not the least happiness from it in truth. Even from their own sons, the wealthy live in fear. This is how it always plays out everywhere.

## The teaching

Shankara is not saying money is evil — he is saying attachment to money as a source of security is self-defeating. The more wealth you accumulate, the more you have to lose, the more you fear loss, and the more suspicious you become — even of those closest to you. Wealth acquired for its own sake creates anxiety, not safety.

## Living this today

Beyond a threshold covering genuine needs and reasonable comfort, additional wealth adds surprisingly little to happiness, while adding considerably to complexity and anxiety. The wealthiest people you know are rarely the most at peace. Yet our systems relentlessly push the message that one more achievement, one more salary band, one more investment will finally produce peace. Notice when you are using financial goals as a proxy for emotional safety. The safety you are seeking cannot be purchased. What you accumulate to feel safe can quietly become the thing you fear losing most.
