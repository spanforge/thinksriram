## The verse

::verse
Praanaayannam Bhajananaama Sankhyaa,
Vedaanta Vakya Manana Nishthaa
Brahmaaham Iti Vibhaavanaa Shuddha,
Bhavati Yatah Pungasya Muktirniyataa
::

## Transliteration

Praanaayannam bhajana-naama sankhyaa / Vedaanta vakya manana nishthaa / Brahmaaham iti vibhaavanaa shuddha / Bhavati yatah pungasya muktir-niyataa

## Translation

Regulated breath, chanting of divine names, contemplation of Vedantic truths, and the pure realisation of "I am Brahman" — from these, liberation is certain.

## The teaching

Having spent most of the text dismantling false refuges, Shankara now offers the positive prescription. Four practices: pranayama (breath regulation), nama japa (repetitive chanting), vedantic contemplation, and the living recognition of one's identity with the absolute. Together, these form an integral path — body, speech, mind, and pure awareness.

## Living this today

Breathwork, meditation, study, and self-inquiry are not ancient luxuries — they are survival tools in a distracted world. Breathwork (pranayama) works quickly and viscerally to shift your inner state — you do not need a study to confirm this, only your next few breaths. Repetitive practice (japa) is what athletes and musicians call deliberate practice. Contemplation of large ideas keeps you oriented toward meaning. And the recognition that you are more than your thoughts and roles — this is the inner stability that no disruption, personal or economic, can take. Begin with five minutes of slow breathing. Today. The breath you take right now is already the beginning of the practice.
