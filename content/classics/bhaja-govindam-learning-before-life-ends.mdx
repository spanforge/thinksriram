## The verse

::verse
Gurucharanaambuja Nirbhara Bhaktah,
Samsaaraat Achiraadbhava Muktah
Sendriya Maanasa Niyamaadevam,
Drakshyasi Nijahridayastham Devam
::

## Transliteration

Guru-charana-ambuja nirbhara bhaktah / Samsaaraat achiraad-bhava muktah / Sendriya maanasa niyamaa-devam / Drakshyasi nija-hridaya-stham devam

## Translation

One who is deeply devoted to the Guru will soon be free from worldly existence. Through disciplined senses and mind, one will see the divine dwelling in one's own heart.

## The teaching

The Guru is central to Vedantic tradition not as an object of blind worship but as a living pointer toward truth. The Guru accelerates the path by providing what no book can: a living example, direct correction, and the transmission of understood experience. Combined with sense-discipline and mental steadiness, one discovers that the divine is not distant — it is the very heart of one's own being.

## Living this today

Today, everyone seems to have an audience and a following, offering themselves as a guide. Shankara is pointing to something different: a genuine mentor relationship — someone who has walked the path you are on, who can see your blind spots because they have navigated their own. This is more valuable than any credential or course. Find your Govindapaada — in your profession, in your inner life, in your creative work. And be willing to be genuinely taught, not just validated. The second half of this verse is the prize: once the mind is disciplined and the senses are regulated, you will encounter the divine not in a temple but in your own awareness. A guide worth following is one who, in time, makes you need them less.
