## The verse

::verse
Praanaayaama Pratyaahaara,
Nityaanitya Vivekavichaara
Jaapyasameta Samaadhibhiaasam,
Kurvavadhaanam Mahadiha Maanas
::

## Transliteration

Praanaayaama pratyaahaara / Nityaanitya viveka-vichaara / Jaapyasameta samaadhi-bhaasam / Kurvavadhaanam mahad-iha maanas

## Translation

Practise pranayama and pratyahara — withdrawal of senses. Engage in discrimination between the eternal and the transient. Along with japa, practise samadhi. Give great attention to the mind.

## The teaching

A technical prescription from the yogic tradition: breath regulation, sense withdrawal, discriminative reasoning, repetitive practice, and states of meditative absorption. The instruction to "give great attention to the mind" is the central teaching — the mind itself must become the object of study.

## Living this today

This verse describes a practice, not a theory, and its effects are easy to notice for yourself. Breathwork steadies the body. Sense withdrawal — deliberately limiting stimulation — restores your capacity to think clearly. Discriminative reasoning is what we now call critical thinking. Japa is deliberate practice. And samadhi — absorbed concentration — is what we now call flow. The mind, the verse insists, must receive your great attention. Yet most people give their mind to anyone who wants it — advertisers, algorithms, newsfeeds — and wonder why they feel scattered and anxious. Take your mind back. Whoever holds your attention is quietly shaping who you become.
