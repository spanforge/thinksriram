## The verse

::verse
Guravam Praaptam Idam Vacho,
Saaram Imam Bhaja Govindam
Yo Jnaatvaatmaanam Svaatmaanam,
Sa Mukto Bhavati Naatra Samshayah
::

## Transliteration

Guravam praaptam idam vacho / Saaram imam bhaja govindam / Yo jnaatva atmanam svaatmanam / Sa mukto bhavati naatra samshayah

## Translation

These words have come from the Guru's wisdom. The essence is: seek Govinda. One who knows the Self by the Self is liberated — there is no doubt about this.

## The teaching

Shankara returns to the refrain — Bhaja Govindam — now framing it as the essence of all teaching. After all the analysis, all the dismantling of illusions, all the practical prescriptions, the message resolves into this simple imperative. And the liberating truth is stated without qualification: self-knowledge is freedom.

## Living this today

All the complexity of self-help, career strategy, relationships, and grief eventually collapses into one question: do you know who you are? Not your credentials, your achievements, or your reputation — but the awareness behind all of these. People who know themselves clearly — their values, their patterns, their actual strengths and actual weaknesses — navigate loss, change, and uncertainty far better than those who have only external anchors. Know yourself. That knowledge is the one thing that cannot be taken from you. Every external anchor eventually gives way. The one that does not is the one you build within.
