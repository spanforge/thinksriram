## The verse

::verse
Sukhapath Cheth Kaschit Smarte Vishnum,
Sthaanaa Sthaanaat Prarthayate Krishnam
Smritvaa Smritvaa Bhajati Bhaktim,
Manasaa Vaachaa Karma Muktim
::

## Transliteration

Sukhapath cheth kaschit smarte vishnum / Sthaanaa sthaanaat prarthayate krishnam / Smritvaa smritvaa bhajati bhaktim / Manasaa vaachaa karma muktim

## Translation

If even by chance one remembers Vishnu with ease, seeking Krishna in every place — through constant remembrance and devotion in thought, word, and deed — liberation comes.

## The teaching

Even accidental, casual remembrance of the divine has transformative power. Shankara does not set a perfectionist bar — even an imperfect, occasional turning toward the sacred is valued. The cultivation of this remembrance across thought, speech, and action is the full path of devotion.

## Living this today

You do not need to be a perfect meditator, a consistent journaller, a flawlessly disciplined person to begin. Even a moment of genuine presence — a breath taken consciously, a pause before you react, a moment of gratitude — is the beginning of transformation. The perfectionist mindset that says "I will begin my practice when I am ready" is the mind's cleverest avoidance strategy. Begin now, imperfectly. Remember your values mid-conversation. Pause before you send the angry message. Look up from the screen for a moment. These small remembrances add up. You do not need to be ready. You only need to begin.
