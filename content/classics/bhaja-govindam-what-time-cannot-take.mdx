## The verse

::verse
Maa Kuru Dhana Jana Yauvana Garvam,
Harati Nimeshaatkaalah Sarvam
Maayaamayam Idamakhilam Hitvaa,
Brahmapadam Tvam Pravisha Viditva
::

## Transliteration

Maa kuru dhana jana yauvana garvam / Harati nimeshaat kaalah sarvam / Maayaamayam idam-akhilam hitvaa / Brahmapadam tvam pravisha viditva

## Translation

Do not take pride in wealth, followers, or youth. Time seizes all of these in a moment. Knowing that this entire world is illusion, enter the abode of Brahman.

## The teaching

Three sources of human pride — money, social standing, and youth — are named and dismissed in one sweep. Time is the great equaliser. No one escapes it. Having acknowledged what time takes, Shankara points to what time cannot touch: the ground of pure being, Brahman. To "enter" it is not to go somewhere — it is to recognise what was always already here.

## Living this today

Reputation, income, and physical vigour are this era's version of this trinity. Your standing, your earnings, your fitness — all will decline. The most admired person of today becomes forgotten in a few years. The most capable performer becomes replaceable when circumstances change. Shankara is not saying do not earn or do not care for your health. He is saying: do not build your dignity on these things, because they will be taken. Build your sense of worth on something that cannot be taken. Your kindness. Your integrity. Your commitment to growth. These are the currencies that compound. Everything time can take was never safe to build your identity upon.
