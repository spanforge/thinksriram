## The verse

::verse
Kurute Gangaasaagara Gamanam,
Vrataparipaalanaathavaa Daanam
Jnaanaviheenah Sarvamatena,
Muktim Na Bhajati Janmashatena
::

## Transliteration

Kurute gangaa-saagara gamanam / Vrata-paripaalana-atha vaa daanam / Jnaana-viheenah sarva-matena / Muktim na bhajati janma-shatena

## Translation

One may go on pilgrimages to the Ganga or the sea, observe fasts and give charity — but without Self-knowledge, liberation does not come even in a hundred births.

## The teaching

Rituals, philanthropy, and religious observance have value — but they are not the destination. Without the transformative clarity of self-knowledge, all external acts are like rearranging furniture in a burning house. Shankara insists that understanding — not performance — is the key.

## Living this today

This verse speaks directly to performative virtue in any era. Public pledges, visible generosity, carefully announced good deeds. None of these are wrong in themselves — but when they substitute for genuine inner transformation, they become sophisticated forms of avoidance. You can donate to ten causes and still be driven by ego, greed, and fear. Genuine change requires self-knowledge: understanding your actual motives, your actual patterns, your actual impact. The pilgrimages of our age are public statements and photographed charity. The jnana Shankara calls for is harder and more private. A good deed announced is already half spent on the announcing.
