## The verse

::verse

Samsaaro'yam Atiiva Vichitrah
Kasya Tvam Kah Kuta Aagatah,
Tattvam Chintaya Tadiha Bhraatah
::

## Transliteration

Kaate kaantaa kaste putraha / Samsaaro'yam atiiva vichitrah / Kasya tvam kah kuta aagatah / Tattvam chintaya tadiha bhraatah

## Translation

Who is your spouse? Who is your son? This world is a strange mystery. Think on the truth: whose are you, who are you, where have you come from?

## The teaching

Shankara poses the three great existential questions directly: Who are you? Where did you come from? To whom do you belong? These are not questions to be answered intellectually but to be lived into. The relationships we consider most defining — spouse, child — are temporary arrangements in a vast and mysterious existence. Wisdom begins with honest bewilderment.

## Living this today

Everyone today has a ready answer to "who are you." You are your job title, your nationality, your affiliations, your preferences. The identity is packaged and presented to others before it is ever examined. But Shankara is pointing to the question beneath the package. Who is the one watching your thoughts? Who is the awareness behind the persona? These are not religious questions — they are psychological ones. The most grounded, resilient people in any era are those who have a stable sense of self that does not depend on external validation. Find out who you are before the world tells you. Before you can be truly seen, you must first be found — by yourself.
