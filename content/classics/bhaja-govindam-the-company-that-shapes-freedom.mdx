## The verse

::verse
Satsangatve Nissangatvam,
Nissangatve Nirmohatvam
Nirmohatve Nishchalatatvam,
Nishchalatve Jivanmuktih
::

## Transliteration

Satsangatve nissangatvam / Nissangatve nirmohatvam / Nirmohatve nishchala-tatvam / Nishchalatve jivanmuktih

## Translation

From good company comes non-attachment; from non-attachment comes freedom from delusion; from freedom from delusion comes steadiness of mind; from steadiness of mind comes liberation while living.

## The teaching

This verse is the only one in Bhaja Govindam that maps a positive path — a step-by-step progression from beginning to liberation. It starts with satsang, good company. The sequence is elegant: right company loosens unhealthy attachments; loosened attachments dissolve delusion; clarity of mind produces steadiness; steadiness is liberation itself.

## Living this today

This is perhaps the most actionable verse in the entire text. You become the average of who you spend time with — this is not self-help cliche, it is ancient wisdom. Your "company" today includes not just the people you sit with, but the voices you let into your mind — what you read, what you watch, who you listen to. Audit ruthlessly. Does your environment pull you toward clarity or toward anxiety? Toward wisdom or toward comparison? The sequence Shankara gives works in reverse too: bad company creates attachment, which creates delusion, which creates restlessness, which creates suffering. You are always being shaped by your environment. Choose it deliberately. Choose your companions carefully. They quietly become your future.
