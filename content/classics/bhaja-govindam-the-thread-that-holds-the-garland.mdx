## The verse

::verse
Kaama Krodho Lobho Mohah,
Tesham Tyaktam Dheeva Bhavaah
Atma Jnaanam Vina Moksho,
Naasti Kimapi Hi Satyam
::

## Transliteration

Kaama krodho lobho mohah / Tesham tyaktam dheeva bhavaah / Atma jnaana vina moksho / Naasti kimapi hi satyam

## Translation

Lust, anger, greed, and delusion — give them up. These are the forest of enemies. Without self-knowledge, there is truly no liberation.

## The teaching

The four classical obstacles — kama (desire), krodha (anger), lobha (greed), moha (delusion) — are explicitly named as the enemies of freedom. The solution is not mere suppression but self-knowledge. You do not escape these forces by willing them away. You transcend them through understanding their nature and your own.

## Living this today

These four are not abstract sins — they show up wherever human attention can be captured. Desire is stirred by what promises pleasure or status. Anger is stirred by what threatens us or offends our sense of fairness. Greed is stirred by scarcity, real or manufactured. Delusion is stirred by whatever confirms what we already believe. You are not weak for being affected — you are human, and these forces have always existed, sharpened further in every age that learns to exploit them. But Shankara's insight holds: the antidote is not willpower but understanding. When you understand how these mechanisms work — in the world around you and in yourself — you gain the freedom to choose differently. What captures your attention today will, in time, come to own it.
