## The verse

::verse
Dinayaaminyau Saayam Praatah,
Shishiravasantau Punaraayaatah
Kaalah Kreedati Gachchhatyaayuh,
Tadapi Na Munchatyaasaavayuh
::

## Transliteration

Dinayaaminyau saayam praatah / Shishira-vasantau punar-aayaatah / Kaalah kreedati gachchhaty-aayuh / Tadapi na munchaty-aashaa-vaayuh

## Translation

Day and night, dusk and dawn, winter and spring — time plays and life ebbs away. Yet the wind of hope does not release its grip.

## The teaching

Time flows with indifferent consistency. The seasons cycle. Life reduces with every rotation. And yet, despite watching this happen, human beings cling to hope — not the wise hope of purposeful action, but the delusional hope that somehow our situation is the exception, that decline will spare us, that there will be time to do what matters later.

## Living this today

"I will do it next year" is the motto of every age. Next year I will take that course. Next year I will spend more time with my family. Next year I will write that book, start that project, have that conversation. But time does not wait for next year. The person whose industry is changing, whose parent is ageing, whose child is growing up faster than expected — none of them have a next year to prepare in. The season is already changing. Shankara's call is for urgency without panic: the urgency of someone who values their time because they know it is finite. There is no next year. There is only this one, disguised as a rehearsal.
