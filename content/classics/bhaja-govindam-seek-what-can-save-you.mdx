## The verse

::verse
Bhaja Govindam, Bhaja Govindam,
Govindam Bhaja Moodhamate
Sampraapte Sannihite Kaale,
Na Hi Na Hi Rakshati Dukrinkarane
::

## Transliteration

Bhaja govindam, bhaja govindam / Govindam bhaja moodhamate / Sampraapte sannihite kaale / Na hi na hi rakshati dukrin-karane

## Translation

Seek Govinda, seek Govinda, seek Govinda, O fool! When the appointed time — death — comes, grammar rules will not save you.

## The teaching

Shankara opens with a thunderclap. He sees an elderly scholar obsessively memorising grammatical rules and cries out in compassion. All your intellectual achievements, credentials, and technical mastery are meaningless at the moment of death. Only devotion to the divine — to something larger than yourself — offers true refuge.

## Living this today

You are the scholar memorising grammar more often than you'd like to admit. You polish your resume, collect certificates, chase the next promotion, refresh your inbox for good news that never quite arrives. Shankara is not against learning — he is against mistaking the map for the territory. Your credentials are not your identity. Every skill you have mastered will one day be surpassed, replaced, or simply forgotten. What remains when the role is gone? Invest daily in something that cannot be taken from you: your inner life, your relationships, your sense of purpose. Seek your Govinda — whatever gives your life meaning beyond your job title. The question is never what you know. The question is what remains when what you know is no longer enough.
