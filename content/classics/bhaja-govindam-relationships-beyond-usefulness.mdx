## The verse

::verse
Yaavadvittopaarjana Saktaha,
Taavannija Parivaro Raktaha
Pashchaajjeevati Jarjara Dehe,
Vaartaam Ko'pi Na Pricchati Gehe
::

## Transliteration

Yaavad-vitto-paarjana saktaha / Taavan-nija parivaro raktaha / Pashchaaj-jeevati jarjara dehe / Vaartaam ko'pi na pricchati gehe

## Translation

As long as you can earn money, your family is devoted to you. When your body weakens in old age, no one in the house even asks about your welfare.

## The teaching

A bitter but honest observation: many relationships are transactional at their core. When you are productive and earning, you are beloved. When you are old, frail, and no longer contributing economically, the warmth fades. Shankara is not preaching cynicism — he is urging us to see clearly, and to build relationships grounded in something deeper than utility.

## Living this today

In an age that rewards networking and self-promotion, this verse is a scalpel. How many of your acquaintances are actually relationships? How many people in your life would show up if you lost everything tomorrow? Uncertainty in work and health keeps forcing this question into the open. Invest in genuine human bonds — not your reputation. Call the friend you have been meaning to call. Spend time with your parents while they are here. Build community, not just career. Your title will not sit beside you when you are ill. A bond that only survives your usefulness was never a bond at all.
