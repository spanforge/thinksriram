## The verse

::verse
Yogarato Vaa Bhogarato Vaa,
Sangarato Vaa Sangataheetah
Yasya Brahmaani Ramate Chittam,
Nandati Nandati Nandatyeva
::

## Transliteration

Yogarato vaa bhogarato vaa / Sangarato vaa sanga-viheenah / Yasya brahmaani ramate chittam / Nandati nandati nandatyeva

## Translation

Whether engaged in yoga or in pleasure, whether in company or in solitude — one whose mind delights in Brahman truly rejoices, rejoices, rejoices.

## The teaching

The penultimate verse reveals the great secret: liberation is not about what you do but about the quality of awareness you bring. Whether you are a yogi or a householder, in community or in solitude, active or contemplative — if your mind rests in the ground of pure being, joy is the natural result. The three-fold repetition of "rejoices" is Shankara's exclamation of wonder.

## Living this today

This is the most liberating verse in the text. It means the path is open to everyone — the busy parent, the working professional, the artist, the monk. You do not need a different life to begin. You need a different quality of presence within your current life. The lawyer who practises with full awareness, the teacher who shows up with genuine attention, the parent who is truly present at dinner — when grounded in the larger awareness, these are already rejoicing. Bring your whole self to wherever you already are. Joy is not found in a different life. It is found in a different way of living this one.
