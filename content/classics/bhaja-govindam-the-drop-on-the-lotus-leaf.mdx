## The verse

::verse
Naliniidalagata Jalamatitaralam,
Tadvajjeevitamatisayachapalam
Viddhi Vyaadhyabhimaana Grastamlokam,
Shokahatamacha Samastam
::

## Transliteration

Nalinii-dala-gata jalam-atitaralam / Tadvaj-jeevitam-atisaya-chapalam / Viddhi vyaadhy-abhimaana-grastam-lokam / Shoka-hatam-cha samastam

## Translation

Life is as trembling and transient as a drop of water on a lotus leaf. Know that the world is consumed by disease, ego, and grief.

## The teaching

A water droplet on a lotus leaf trembles with every breath of wind, on the verge of falling at any moment. Our lives are exactly this — vivid, trembling, and brief. Shankara is not being pessimistic; he is being precise. Recognising impermanence is not despair — it is the beginning of wisdom.

## Living this today

Every generation is taught this lesson anew, usually by loss: everything can change overnight. Health, work, relationships, entire ways of life — gone in an illness, an accident, a single piece of news. What seemed permanent turns out never to have been. The correct response is not anxiety, but presence. The water droplet is beautiful precisely because it is temporary. Do not postpone living your actual life while waiting for the "right" moment of stability. That moment is a myth. It was always a myth. Impermanence is not life's flaw. It is life's greatest teacher.
