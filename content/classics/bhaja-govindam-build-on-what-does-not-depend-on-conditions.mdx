## Transliteration

Vayasi gate kah kaama-vikaarah / Shushke neere kah kaasaarah / Kshiine vitte kah parivaarah / Jnaate tattve kah samsaarah

## Translation

When youth has passed, where is lust? When the lake has dried, where is it? When wealth is gone, where are the relatives? When truth is known, where is the world of illusion?

## The teaching

Four rhetorical questions that reveal the conditional nature of all worldly experience. Desire depends on youth; beauty depends on water; loyalty depends on wealth; the illusion of the world depends on ignorance. Remove the condition, and the experience vanishes. The only thing that does not depend on conditions is the truth itself.

## Living this today

Everything in your current life is conditional. Your influence depends on your relevance. Your income depends on circumstances beyond your control. Your social circle depends on proximity and shared interest. This is not a pessimistic observation — it is liberating. If you build your identity on conditional things, you will live in constant anxiety about those conditions changing. Build something unconditional: your values, your character, your capacity for presence. These survive setbacks, loss, and change. When the ground shifts beneath your work or your world, who you are at your core is what remains. Nothing conditional was ever meant to hold your whole weight.
