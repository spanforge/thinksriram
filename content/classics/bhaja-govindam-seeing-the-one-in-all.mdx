## The verse

::verse
Geyam Gitaa Naama Sahasram,
Dhyeyam Shreepati Roopam Ajasram
Neyam Sajjana Sange Chittam,
Deyam Daridraya Eva Vittam
::

## Transliteration

Geyam gitaa naama sahasram / Dhyeyam shree-pati roopam ajasram / Neyam sajjana sange chittam / Deyam daaridraya eva vittam

## Translation

Chant the Gita and the thousand names of the Lord. Meditate always on the form of the divine. Guide the mind toward the company of the good. Give your wealth to the needy.

## The teaching

A four-fold practical programme: scriptural engagement, meditation, good company, and generous giving. This is Shankara's positive prescription after all the warnings — not a list of prohibitions but a vision of a well-lived life. Notably, charity is included not as a moral obligation but as a natural overflow of a wisdom-oriented life.

## Living this today

Translate this for today: read something meaningful, not just useful. Meditate — even five minutes daily. Curate your company toward wisdom and genuine goodness. And give — money, time, skill — to those with less. Every honest account of a flourishing life keeps circling back to some version of these four elements: meaningful engagement, contemplative practice, community, and contribution. Shankara arrived at the same formula twelve centuries ago, simply by paying close attention. These are not religious recommendations; they are perennial truths about human thriving. A well-lived life is rarely complicated. It is simply rarely practised.
