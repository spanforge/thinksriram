## The verse

::verse
Rathyaa Chapara Vibhushita Vesho,
Harati Na Chitham Prabhoh Sandesho
Maanas Sanaatana Paaravaare,
Paribhramati Kila Taraanga Bhaare
::

## Transliteration

Rathyaa chapara vibhushita vesho / Harati na chitham prabhoh sandesho / Maanas sanaatana paaravaare / Paribhramati kila taraanga bhaare

## Translation

Clothed in rags or adorned in finery, the Lord's message stirs the heart. The mind wanders on the timeless ocean, amid the weight of waves.

## The teaching

Whether a person is dressed in a beggar's rags or royal finery, the call of the divine touches all equally. The external condition is irrelevant to inner receptivity. But the mind, like a boat on a vast ocean, is constantly tossed by waves of thought and emotion — and so even when the call comes, it is often drowned out.

## Living this today

The divine call — the call to your deeper self, your authentic purpose, your most meaningful work — is speaking to you regardless of your circumstances. It does not require you to be rich, successful, or spiritually advanced. But you will not hear it if your mind is in perpetual agitation. The ocean of distraction in any busy life — obligations, worries, chatter, comparison — generates waves that drown the signal. Stillness is not a luxury; it is the prerequisite for hearing what matters. Create space in your day where the waves can settle. The call was never silent. Only the mind was too loud to hear it.
