First principles thinking asks you to stop borrowing conclusions and start examining foundations.

Many decisions are built on assumptions we inherited from habit, culture, or convenience.

## Rebuild from what remains

Ask what must be true. Remove what is merely familiar. Rebuild from the ground that remains.

> The simplest truth is often quieter than the loudest assumption.

This does not make thinking easy. It makes thinking clean.
