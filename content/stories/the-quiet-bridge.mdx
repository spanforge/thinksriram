The bridge was narrow, old, and quiet. Two travelers reached it from opposite sides.

Each believed the other should wait.

## The crossing

They argued until the sun moved. Then one traveler noticed the other was carrying a child.

The facts had not changed. The frame had.

> Understanding often begins when the other person's burden becomes visible.

Most conflicts are not solved by winning the bridge. They are softened by seeing who is trying to cross it.
